# Pass/fail splits the machine from the product

## Insight
Split the verdict by funnel layer. Acquisition is the machine's job, retention is the product's job. The thesis-vindicating quadrant is efficient acquisition plus poor retention: keep the machine, change the product, trigger Brand 2.

## Why it holds
Top of funnel transfers to any product: CPA compression, creative win rate, cold conversion. Bottom of funnel is product-specific: repeat rate, subscription retention, reviews, organic pull. The operating system is the asset, so the gate must measure the machine's transferability, not just Brand 1 P&L. The layers bleed, and small-n makes retention noisy, so weight the decision toward the machine and treat retention as directional.

## So what
Machine passes if blended CPA falls toward £30 or below, a repeatable share of hooks beat threshold, cold conversion is acceptable, and the infrastructure is cheap, fast, reusable. Product passes if second-order rate holds, subscription retention tracks 22 to 7 percent or better, sentiment is positive, and refund rate is low. This quadrant is the scaffold for the no-deviation playbook gate.
