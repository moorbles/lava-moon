# Brand 2 is ring-fenced from Brand 1 operating revenue, now at an explicit figure

## Insight
A defined emergency floor for Brand 2 is funded from Brand 1 operating revenue, not held as a separate launch fund. Even if Brand 1 fails, enough capital must remain to attempt Brand 2.

The explicit figure, previously an open item, is now set in Budget Model v4.2: **£2,000** (Settings, row 9: "Brand 2 emergency ring-fence"). Total founder investment is £11,500 (row 8), so the Brand 1 envelope is £9,500 (£11,500 − £2,000) — see FIN-004 for the full reconciliation.

## Why it holds
The thesis is multi-brand. A single brand's failure should not end the operating-system experiment. Ring-fencing affects how aggressively capital is deployed in phase one. Peak drawdown in the base case still sits inside the £11,500 raise, with £2,000 of it untouchable by Brand 1.

## So what
Treat £2,000 as the hard floor in the playbook gate. Brand 1 spend must not breach £9,500 (£9,800 including the smoke test, which sits outside the v4.2 model — see FIN-004) without a gate decision to draw down the Brand 2 floor.
