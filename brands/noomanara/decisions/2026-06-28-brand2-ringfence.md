# Brand 2 is ring-fenced from Brand 1 operating revenue

## Insight
A defined emergency floor for Brand 2 is funded from Brand 1 operating revenue, not held as a separate launch fund. Even if Brand 1 fails, enough capital must remain to attempt Brand 2.

## Why it holds
The thesis is multi-brand. A single brand's failure should not end the operating-system experiment. Ring-fencing affects how aggressively capital is deployed in phase one. Peak drawdown in the base case still sits inside the £11,500 raise.

## So what
Treat the ring-fence as a cash-survival constraint in the playbook gate. Do not deploy into Brand 1 in a way that breaches the floor. The explicit figure is an open item from the founder call.

## To validate
Locked principle. Set the explicit ring-fence figure.
