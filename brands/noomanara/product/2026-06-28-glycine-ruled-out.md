# Glycine ruled out as a standalone SKU

## Insight
Glycine is a large, real, growing ingredient with genuine sleep science, but it is a commodity with no meaningful brand differentiation. It is a poor fit for a premium DTC brand. Magnesium glycinate is the more viable single-ingredient SKU candidate if the brand later pursues that format.

## Why it holds
Amazon glycine is a price-per-gram race dominated by BulkSupplements, NOW, Nutricost. Premium DTC positioning needs a defensible ingredient story, which glycine cannot carry. The mushroom category is superior precisely because sourcing, extraction ratios, and certifications give defensible differentiation. Note: many published glycine market-size figures online appear to be low-quality AI-generated content and should be treated with heavy skepticism.

## So what
Do not build a glycine SKU. If a single-ingredient line is ever pursued, magnesium glycinate is the candidate, consistent with its removal from the stack (PRD-001). Differentiation is sourcing and creative, not the molecule.
