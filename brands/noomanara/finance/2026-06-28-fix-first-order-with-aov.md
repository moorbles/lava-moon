# Fix first-order economics with AOV, not subscription retention

## Insight
A first order priced as a single £35 bottle is underwater. Fix it with a bigger basket, not by hoping subscription retention rescues it. At the 2-bottle-led base mix the first order recovers to roughly breakeven, plus £0.78 per customer excluding CAC at base CPA.

## Why it holds
Churn and subscribe rate move the result comparatively little. Mix and CPA are the swing variables. Leaning on retention to rescue an underwater acquisition is the common DTC mistake. A break-even-to-slightly-negative first order is the category norm, not a failure.

## So what
Merchandising for AOV is the primary first-order lever. The 2-bottle entry default is the mechanism. Canonical numbers live in the v4.2 model, not here. This insight points to the model, it does not restate it.

## To validate
Validated in the model. The inputs behind it, mix and CPA, are confirmed only by live traffic and the smoke test.
