# Stock-formula route is the validation path, now confirmed at 500-unit MOQ

## Insight
Use the stock-formula route to validate: find a manufacturer whose existing formula is close enough and white-label it. Lower MOQ than custom, faster to market, lower cost. Custom formulation is the post-PMF path.

The MOQ question parked on the founder call is now resolved: **500 units**, not the 100–200 originally assumed in early planning. This is the live figure in Budget Model v4.2 (Settings, row 26: Units ordered (MOQ) = 500; COGS per bottle = £5; 25 units gifted for seeding; 475 sellable units; ~232 implied first orders from the batch).

## Why it holds
Custom at 500-plus units pushes inventory toward £3,000-plus and consumes most of the budget before any creative is tested. The point of the validation test is to learn whether creative and positioning work, which does not require a bespoke formula. Save custom for after product-market fit is confirmed.

The 500-unit figure is no longer a risk to plan around — it is the model's working assumption, and the inventory cost (500 × £5 = £2,500) and seeding allowance are already built into the v4.2 cost and inventory block.

## So what
First conversations ask for the closest existing white-label sleep formula at or near 500-unit MOQ, then custom MOQ as a fallback. The founder-call tension (raise media spend to clear a larger stock position, or accept the constraint and look at alternative routes) is resolved in favour of accepting 500 units and pacing ad spend to clear it — that pacing is reflected in the v4.2 media plan and P&L.

## Drift flag
The launch bible's decisions register still states the old assumption: D2 ("Lower MOQ (100–200 units), 4–6 wk lead, ~£4–5/unit") and D5 ("Place 150 units"). Both predate the 500-unit confirmation and need correcting — see DEC-003 for the full register drift flag.

## To validate
Validated at 500 units in the model. Open: does a stock formula exist close enough to the locked four-ingredient spec at that MOQ — confirm with shortlisted suppliers (Tier 1: Life Lab, Nutrikal).
