# Agreed SHA terms (being formalised via SeedLegals)

## Insight
The substantive 50/50 SHA terms agreed in the drafting session.

- Deadlock: two layers. Domain-split authority for operational decisions (Matt holds commercial, tech, finance; Isabel holds creative, content, programme management). Russian roulette as the exit mechanism.
- Vesting: 3-year schedule, 6-month cliff, monthly thereafter.
- Leaver: defined good and bad leaver categories. Bad leaver gets nominal value on unvested shares.
- Transfer: standard restrictions and pre-emption rights.
- IP: assigned to the company.
- Non-compete: narrow, focused on customer and supplier solicitation and misuse of confidential information, not broad market restriction.
- Drawings: no salaries or dividends until two consecutive cashflow-positive months, after which drawings become a joint reserved matter.
- M6 divergence: a financial-threshold trigger, not a calendar date or equity ratchet. Once the company sustains net profit for two monthly drawings for two consecutive months, the second founder commits to joining within a defined window. The all-in founder draws a salary in the gap period. Equity stays 50/50.
- Longstop: M36 to revisit the whole structure if the threshold is never reached.

## Why it holds
These were worked through deliberately and map to the M6-to-M24 gap structure the founders intend. The financial-threshold M6 trigger is the same mechanism recorded in DEC-007.

## So what
This is the agreed-terms record. It partially answers the failure-scenario gaps flagged on the founder call (LEG-002): Russian roulette covers deadlock, the M36 longstop covers the never-reached case. The remaining open work is formalising all of it in the SeedLegals SHA and reconciling the vesting discrepancy in the launch bible (LEG-003).
