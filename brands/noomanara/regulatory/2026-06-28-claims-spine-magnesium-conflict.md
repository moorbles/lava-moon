# The compliant claims spine was built on magnesium, which is now cut

## Insight
There are no authorised GB health claims for lion's mane, reishi, or ashwagandha. Magnesium does carry authorised claims: normal nervous-system function and reduction of tiredness and fatigue. The earlier plan was to build the compliant spine of the copy on magnesium. Magnesium has since been removed from the formula (PRD-001). The compliant claims spine no longer has an ingredient to stand on.

## Why it holds
Authorised claims attach to specific ingredients at specific doses. Remove the ingredient and you lose the right to make its claims. The ASA has ruled against unauthorised claims in this category, so the copy cannot simply assert benefits for the mushrooms instead.

## So what
This is an unresolved tension, not a solved decision. Either the copy strategy moves to structure-function and lifestyle language with no authorised-claim spine, or magnesium is reconsidered, or a different authorised-claim ingredient is added. Resolve before the claims review. Flagging it is the point. Do not let the copy inherit a magnesium-based spine that the formula no longer supports.
