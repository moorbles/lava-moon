# Set a 60-day minimum commitment as the product promise

## Insight
Frame the product around a 60-day trial. The evidence supports giving it eight weeks, long enough for the primary mushroom mechanisms to operate while staying a commercially reasonable trial window.

## Why it holds
The actives accumulate. They are not same-night effects. Eight weeks is where the slower pathways have had time to work. The promise also lines up with the locked offer: the 60-day guarantee and the 2-bottle entry pack are the same unit (see DEC-002, DEC-001-offer).

## So what
Make 60 days the explicit promise in onboarding and on pack. It reinforces the 2-bottle pack as the cold-entry default. A single 30-day bottle undersells the mechanism and forces a reorder decision exactly when the product is starting to work.

## To validate
Confirm repeat-purchase clusters at or after day 60 in live data. If churn spikes before day 60 despite the promise, the onboarding sequence is not landing the expectation. See CRM-003.
