# Churn is a two-stage cliff, not a flat rate

## Insight
Supplement churn is roughly 20 to 25 percent across the first shipment, then 5 to 8 percent monthly once the month-two-to-four cliff passes. The second shipment is the single biggest predictor of long-term retention, and it is mostly determined by acquisition and onboarding quality.

## Why it holds
Customers trial, then a large share drop after the first order. The ones who clear the cliff retain at a far lower steady rate. Modelling a flat churn rate hides the cliff and overstates early retention.

## So what
Model and plan to the two-stage curve, not a flat rate. Because the second shipment is set by acquisition and onboarding, the retention levers are upstream: expectation framing (CRM-004), the education sequence (CRM-003), and not over-promising in creative. Bulk packs that carry the customer past the cliff are retention-protective (see CRM-006).

## To validate
Track first-to-second order rate as the leading retention signal. The model assumes 22 percent first-shipment churn and 7 percent steady. Confirm against live cohorts.
