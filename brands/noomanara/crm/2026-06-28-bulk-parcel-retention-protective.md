# Bulk single-parcel shipping is retention-protective

## Insight
Ship every pack as one parcel. Do not split into monthly shipments. Bulk packs carry the customer past the 30-day onset before any reorder decision. A monthly drip forces that decision at day 30, the efficacy trough, which is the cliff.

## Why it holds
The product takes about 30 days to work. A monthly shipment makes the customer decide to continue at the exact point they have felt least. A 60-day pack clears the onset first. Monthly shipping also costs an estimated £1.1k to £3.3k of year-one cash in postage and roughly triples Isabel's fulfilment labour, for a benefit that disclosure already delivers.

## So what
Locked: bulk, one parcel. The are-they-aware worry is a disclosure problem, fixed with clear checkout labelling, a what-to-expect onboarding email, a pre-renewal reminder, and card-update dunning. Not a fulfilment-structure problem.

## To validate
Locked decision. No validation required. Revisit only if a monthly-drip SKU is added as a secondary option.
