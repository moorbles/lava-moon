# Be explicit about what feeling it means for a sleep product

## Insight
The felt effect is not being knocked out. It is falling asleep slightly more easily, waking less at 3am, and feeling marginally less wired at bedtime. Customers who expect a sleeping-pill effect churn. Customers who know to look for subtle improvements notice them and stay.

## Why it holds
Expectation sets the frame the customer judges the product through. If the implicit promise is sedation, the real effect reads as failure. If the promise is calibrated to subtle, real improvements, the same effect reads as success. The churn is driven by mismatched expectation, not by the product underperforming.

## So what
Define feeling it explicitly in onboarding. Name the three signals: easier onset, fewer night wakes, less wired at bedtime. Do not let acquisition creative imply a knockout effect to win the click. It buys a customer primed to churn. The expectation set in the ad must match the expectation managed in onboarding.

## To validate
Watch review language for the knockout-expectation pattern in early cohorts. If reviews say it did nothing, check whether acquisition creative over-promised before assuming a product fault.
