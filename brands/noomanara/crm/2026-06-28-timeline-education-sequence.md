# Build a customer education sequence around the timeline

## Insight
An email or app flow at days 3, 14, 30, and 60 that explains why the customer may not feel much yet, and what to look for, is one of the highest-ROI retention tools in the category. AG1 and Ritual built significant LTV on this model. The GTM analysis estimates a timeline education sequence can lift 90-day retention by 20 to 30 percent.

## Why it holds
The product works on a delay. Left unmanaged, the gap between purchase and felt effect is where customers conclude it is not working and cancel. A sequenced flow reframes the silence as the expected path and tells the customer what subtle signals to watch for at each stage. Competitors do not explain this, which is the opening.

## So what
Build the four-touch flow in Klaviyo. Day 3 sets the early-calm expectation (CRM-002). Day 14 and day 30 manage the still-subtle phase and define what improvement looks like (CRM-004). Day 60 lands the full promise and the reorder or upgrade moment. This is the mechanism that makes the 60-day promise survive a customer who feels nothing in week one.

## To validate
Hypothesis until built and measured. Compare 60 and 90-day retention for customers who get the flow versus a holdout. The metric that matters is repeat-purchase rate.
